What is AYUSH?

AYUSH stands for Ayurveda, Yoga & Naturopathy, Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy. It represents the traditional systems of medicine practiced in India, focusing on holistic health and well-being by integrating body, mind, and spirit.

Ayurveda

Ayurveda, the "Science of Life," is an ancient Indian system of medicine that emphasizes balance in the body through diet, herbal remedies, meditation, and yoga. It aims to promote longevity and vitality by addressing the root cause of diseases and imbalances rather than just treating symptoms.

Yoga & Naturopathy

Yoga is a spiritual and ascetic discipline that includes breath control, simple meditation, and the adoption of specific bodily postures. It is widely practiced for health and relaxation. Naturopathy focuses on natural remedies and the body's ability to heal and maintain itself through treatments like fasting, nutrition, and hydrotherapy.

Unani

Unani medicine, also known as Unani Tibb, is a traditional system of healing and health maintenance observed in South Asia. It is based on the teachings of Hippocrates and Galen and focuses on the balance of bodily fluids or humors to maintain health. Treatments include herbal medicine, dietary practices, and lifestyle modifications.

Siddha

The Siddha system is one of the oldest traditional medicines in the world, originating in South India. It is based on the principles of natural healing and emphasizes a holistic approach to treating the mind, body, and soul through alchemy, herbal medicine, and lifestyle practices.
